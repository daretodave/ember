describe the weather as if you were writing a letter to someone who lives nowhere near you.

today's tiny task — eat one meal without a screen.

the city is between weathers this week. mornings are still cold enough that the cars steam up at the lights, but by noon i'm carrying my coat instead of wearing it. the river is fast — there's been rain north of here, and it comes through. yesterday i sat on the bench by the bridge and watched a piece of someone's notebook page go past in the current, end over end, faster than anyone walking on the path.

i think about how spring here is mostly a negotiation between sun and wind. neither of them is winning. neither of them is settling for it.

i hope wherever you are is making up its mind, or has, and that you're warm enough either way.
